## How to use this list

Start with two or three foods you can realistically repeat this week. Gut-friendly eating usually works better when it comes from steady habits: a bit more fiber, a bit more plant variety, enough fluids, and a few fermented foods if they suit you.

The foods below are here because they are practical, widely available, and easy to fit into normal meals. Oats, lentils, chickpeas, fruit, leafy greens, yogurt, kefir, and fermented vegetables are not special because they are trendy. They are useful because they make everyday eating a little more supportive and a lot easier to repeat.

## Simple ways to start

- Add oats or seeds to breakfast for a steady fiber base.
- Use yogurt or kefir as a simple fermented food if dairy works for you.
- Add one fruit and one vegetable to meals you already eat.
- Use beans or lentils a few times per week instead of trying to change every meal at once.

If you have a diagnosed digestive condition or a food intolerance, treat this page as general food guidance rather than medical advice, and adjust portions based on your own tolerance.
