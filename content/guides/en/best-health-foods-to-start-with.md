## Why start with these foods?

The best foods to start with are usually not the most unusual ones. They are the foods you can find easily, use more than once, and fit into meals you already know how to make. That makes them much more useful than foods that sound healthy but rarely make it into your routine.

The foods below are a strong starting group because they cover the basics well. Some help with breakfast, some make better snacks, some make lunch and dinner more filling, and some improve the overall quality of meals in a quiet but important way.

## What makes a food a good starting point

- It is easy to buy and easy to keep around.
- It fits into more than one kind of meal.
- It adds something useful, such as fiber, protein, healthy fats, or better meal structure.
- It is realistic enough that you can keep eating it next week, not just today.

You do not need to start with every food on the page. Pick a few that match the meals you actually eat now, then add more variety once those choices start to feel normal.
