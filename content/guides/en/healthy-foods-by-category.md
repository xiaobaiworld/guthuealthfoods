## Why browse by category at all?

Looking at every food one by one is not always the most useful way to start. Most people are not trying to redesign their entire diet for gut health in one day. They are usually trying to fix one part of it: better breakfasts, better snacks, more vegetables, or more reliable lunch and dinner staples.

That is where categories help. They let you focus on one cluster of foods at a time, which usually makes food decisions feel less random and more practical.

## What each category is useful for

- **Fruits** are often the easiest place to start when snacks feel weak or overly processed.
- **Leafy greens** help when meals feel low in vegetables or low in volume.
- **Legumes and grains** are useful when you want more filling meals, more fiber, or a better breakfast base.
- **Healthy oils** matter because cooking fats influence everyday meals more than people often realize.
- **Functional foods** are worth looking at when you want practical fermented foods or nutrient-dense staples that fit into normal eating patterns.
- **Drinks** can matter more than expected, especially if sugar-heavy beverages or low-protein drinks show up often.

## How to use these category pages well

Start with the category that matches the part of your diet you are most likely to improve first. If breakfast is the weak point, grains, yogurt-style foods, and fruit are usually more useful than reading about everything at once. If dinners feel repetitive, vegetables, legumes, and cooking oils are often a better starting point.

The goal here is not to master the whole site in one sitting. It is to find the next few foods that make your daily meals a little easier to build and a little easier to repeat.
