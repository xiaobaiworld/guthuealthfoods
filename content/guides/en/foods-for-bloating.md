## A calmer way to choose foods

When bloating is frequent, a simpler meal pattern is often easier to test than a long list of restrictions. Start with familiar foods, smaller portions, and fewer ingredients in one meal, then adjust based on how you feel.

The foods below are included because they can help you build meals that are easier to repeat and easier to observe. Oats, brown rice, cooked greens, yogurt, kefir, kiwi, ginger, and olive oil can all fit into a calmer routine without turning eating into a project.

## How to test the list

- Try one new food at a time instead of changing the whole diet at once.
- Prefer cooked vegetables if raw salads feel uncomfortable.
- Keep portions moderate, especially with beans, dairy, or high-fiber foods.
- Track what you ate and how you felt for a few days before judging a food.

Persistent or painful bloating can have many causes. This page is food guidance, not a diagnosis, so seek professional care if symptoms are severe, new, or getting worse.
