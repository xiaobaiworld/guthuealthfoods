## Our Mission

Our mission is simple: make trustworthy, science-backed nutrition knowledge available equally in English and Chinese. Reliable health information has long been unevenly distributed across languages. English-language nutrition resources are often more numerous, more detailed, and more frequently updated than comparable Chinese resources, and that gap matters for readers who rely on Chinese as their primary language.

We built Gut Health Foods to help close that gap. Every food profile is researched, written, and reviewed with the goal of being genuinely useful in both languages. We do not want one language to feel like the “main” version and the other to feel secondary.

## What We Cover and Why

We focus on whole foods and minimally processed ingredients: the foods that consistently appear in evidence-based eating patterns linked with long-term health. We do not profile supplements, diet programs, or branded products. Our view is that the most useful nutrition knowledge begins with ordinary foods eaten every day, such as vegetables, fruits, legumes, whole grains, healthy fats, herbs, spices, and drinks.

Our eight food categories were chosen to cover the core food groups most often connected with chronic disease prevention, metabolic health, and overall dietary quality.

## Our Sourcing Approach

We use peer-reviewed nutrition research, systematic reviews, meta-analyses, and guidance from recognized nutrition and public health organizations. When we describe a benefit, we try to explain both the outcome and the reason behind it. We also distinguish clearly between strong, well-established evidence and findings that are still emerging.

We update content when meaningful new evidence changes the bigger picture. Our goal is not to chase every headline study, but to keep the site accurate, current, and grounded in durable nutritional science.
